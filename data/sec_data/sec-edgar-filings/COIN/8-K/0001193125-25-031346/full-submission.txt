(the &#8220;Company&#8221;) and Coinbase, Inc. (together with the Company, &#8220;Coinbase&#8221;), alleging that Coinbase, Inc. had acted as an unregistered securities exchange, broker, and clearing agency in violation of Sections 5, 15(a) and 17A(b) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, as amended (the &#8220;Exchange Act&#8221;), and that, through its Staking program, Coinbase, Inc. had offered and sold securities without registering its offers and sales in violation of Sections 5(a) and 5(c) of the Securities Act of 1933, as amended (the &#8220;Securities Act&#8221;). The SEC also alleged that the Company was liable for the alleged violations as an alleged control person of Coinbase, Inc. The case is captioned<span style="font-style:italic">&#160;SEC v.
